Grundig Box550 recreate

Recreating classic grunding professional box 550 speakers boxes

I am fond of Grundig music systems and accessories manufactured till 80's. My father purshased Grundig "CN 930", Cassette recording system for starting a recording shop (LP's to Cassettes) That was a big thing back then in a Bikaner, Rajasthan (India) where I used to live during schooling. Years passed and in 2005, i found out my dad still has the CN 930 in working condition carrying only few minor scratches proudly boasting its usage. With minor repairs like changing belt and pinch roller, the system was ready to run. I always heard from my father and brothers that it sounds good, but I was truly amazed when I heard it for the firs time, it blew me complitely, it was far better then any high end cd player, the sound quality was mesmerizing. Value of tapes suddenly rose for me. Coun'd help but thought if the player sound so good with a unbranded amplifer and unmatched speakers. How it would sound if it has compatible amp and speakers?

Years went by, I was earning myself and facebook & ebay was accessible to Indians. I got coonnected with a facebook page grundiglove.org. Where I asked if somebody can guide me on compatible amp and speakers for "CN 930". Luca Visentini quickly helped me out in sharing a wonderful mapping of grundig products with compatibile speakers, amps and other players (turntables, spool players, type of speakers). Without wasting much time I started looking at ebay.de if I can find amp and speakers, I luckly managed to find a good deal of "Studio 2220" amp from 1975-76 as a spare part but repaired eventually (part of another blog). But it was very difficult and highly expensive to buy speakers, after few futile attempts and waiting for almost an year. I decided to buy only drivers which are availble as parts and thought to recreate boxes.
The drivers which I purchased were for Grundig 350 which were good match with amplifier and player but their boxes were quite different aestheticlly. The closes aethetcially boxes were Grundig 550a which was professional series. I decided to take aesthetic elements from 550a and build boxes of physical size 350. Once i recieved the drivers, i quickly checked the dimensions and checked https://www.radiomuseum.org/r/grundig_2_weg_lautsprecher_box_350b.html for dimensions, I referred hundres of pictures of 550a for matching the asethtics. Created CAD drawings and 3D printed aesthetic elements of box550a and laser cutted wooden box of same dimension and thickness wood as box350.
After glueing the box, I pasted a black vinyl wrap to the boxes and suddenly they started looking great. The fancy grills and datasheet of drivers were still pending.
I ordered passive crossevers for the drivers and recieved delayed, meanwhile the boxes were already made. To my surprise the crossever circuit came with speaker wire connectors as well, I decided to put the boxes under laser cut and create whole to fit the connector as well (Orignal plan was to let the wires coming out from box)
Finally it was time to test it wheather the acoustics are good or bad though the dimensions of box and drivers are a close match but crossovers plays a big role in assigning frequencies to drivers. Bang!.. The sound was better than i expected, It is a sealed box design hence it has a punchy bass which i like (doesn't go beyond 70hz because the driver itself 4-1/2inch only), but it has clear midtones and sharp high frequencies. It was time to move to asthetics, I recreated speaker rings painted them with powder aluminium coat, recreated frequency curves shown on the boxes and printed them on silver sheet. Once all the fancy grills and other things were rightly placed, the speakers started looking asthetically similar to box 550a and the design language was matching with Studio 2220 & CN 930.
